## Important notice

This draft is not an effective contract and is not legal advice. It must not be
published as binding terms until a responsible legal entity, governing law,
jurisdiction, support/legal contacts, age/eligibility policy, product scope,
consumer-law review and acceptance/version mechanism are approved.

## 1. Scope

These draft terms would govern access to the YNX Chain informational website,
YNX Testnet documentation, developer tools and specifically identified Testnet
products. Product-specific terms must control where custody, exchange, payments,
stablecoins, cards, managed strategies, storage, communications or other
regulated/high-risk services are introduced.

YNX Chain is a Web4 Layer-1 ecosystem. YNX Web4 is the application ecosystem.
YNXT is the native Testnet asset. YNX Testnet uses EVM Chain ID 6423.

## 2. Testnet status

YNX Testnet is for testing. Testnet assets may have no monetary value, may be
reset or changed, may be unavailable and are not guaranteed to convert to any
future asset. Testnet use does not establish Mainnet launch, production custody,
issuer support, exchange liquidity, returns, reserves or regulatory approval.

Users must not buy, sell or borrow against Testnet assets based on an expectation
of value created by this draft.

## 3. Eligibility

Before effectiveness, counsel must define minimum age, authority to contract,
excluded/sanctioned jurisdictions, restricted persons, business-user authority
and any product-specific KYC/KYB requirements. No draft language waives mandatory
consumer rights.

## 4. Accounts and security

Users are responsible for safeguarding approved account credentials and devices.
YNX support, AI and documentation will not request a seed phrase or private key.
Users must verify network, recipient, amount, fee, scope and expiry before signing.

Product sessions are bounded by product, device, account, scope and expiry.
Users must report suspected compromise through the approved security channel once
that channel is established.

## 5. Acceptable use

Users must comply with the separately reviewed Acceptable Use Policy. Prohibited
uses include unauthorized access, malware, credential theft, fraud, sanctions
evasion, market manipulation, abusive automation, privacy violations, unlawful
content, interference with consensus/services and attempts to bypass limits or
security controls.

## 6. User content and developer submissions

Before effectiveness, each product must define ownership, license scope,
moderation, removal, export, deletion, feedback and open-source contribution
terms. A submission license must be limited to operating and improving the chosen
service and must not silently grant rights to unrelated content or private data.

Users must have necessary rights and must not submit secrets, personal data of
others without authority, malware or infringing material.

## 7. Transactions and fees

Blockchain transactions may be irreversible after commitment. Interfaces must
show fees and material risks before approval. Provider, venue, protocol, compute,
data, subscription, management or performance fees require product-specific
disclosure. Hidden spread and fees on unrealized profit are prohibited by policy.

An accepted gateway response is not finality. Unknown outcomes require
authoritative verification before retry.

## 8. Wallet, custody and third parties

Unless a product-specific agreement explicitly states otherwise, users control
their Wallet and YNX does not take custody. Third-party wallets, venues,
providers, bridges, issuers, custodians and websites have separate terms and risks.
YNX does not endorse or guarantee them merely by documenting an integration.

No current draft establishes production custody, official stablecoin, live
Bridge, banking, card issuing, insurance or investment management.

## 9. AI-assisted features

AI output may be inaccurate, incomplete or unavailable and is not authoritative
chain, legal, financial, medical or regulatory advice. AI may draft or propose but
cannot independently sign, pay, trade, publish, delete, change permissions,
operate Treasury or upgrade consensus. Users review and approve applicable output.

## 10. Availability and changes

Testnet and candidate services may change, pause, migrate or stop. Versioned
notices should describe material changes, compatibility, export/exit, retention
and sunset. Mandatory notice/consent rights depend on the approved jurisdiction
and product.

## 11. Intellectual property

YNX names, logos, documentation and software are subject to their identified
rights and licenses. Open-source components retain their licenses. These draft
terms do not grant rights to another organization’s marks or imply affiliation.

## 12. Disclaimers

Subject to mandatory law and final counsel review, Testnet services would be
provided for evaluation without promises of uninterrupted availability,
compatibility, security, value, liquidity, profit or fitness for a regulated
purpose. No disclaimer may exclude liability that law does not permit excluding.

## 13. Limitation of liability

No limitation is proposed as final. Counsel must determine enforceable categories,
caps, carve-outs and consumer treatment for each jurisdiction and product,
including fraud, willful misconduct, confidentiality, privacy, security, IP,
custody, payment and statutory liabilities.

## 14. Indemnity

No indemnity is proposed as final. Any business-user indemnity requires scope,
causation, defense control, settlement consent, exclusions and mandatory-law
review. Consumer indemnity may be inappropriate or unenforceable.

## 15. Suspension and termination

Final terms must define objective grounds, emergency action, notice where lawful,
appeal, correction, data/asset export, withdrawal/redemption, retention and
service-sunset rights. Suspension must not silently erase evidence or user assets.

## 16. Disputes and governing law

Legal entity, governing law, venue, arbitration/class-action treatment, informal
resolution and consumer regulator/ombudsman rights are not selected. They require
jurisdiction-specific counsel and must not be filled from an engineering template.

## 17. Contact and notices

Support, legal, privacy, security and status contacts/URLs are not approved. The
terms cannot become effective until durable channels and incident/escalation
ownership exist.

## 18. Acceptance checklist

- Responsible legal entity and address.
- Governing law, venue and consumer-law analysis.
- Product and jurisdiction scope.
- Eligibility and sanctions policy.
- Support/legal/privacy/security contacts.
- Privacy notice and AUP alignment.
- Fee, custody, transaction and third-party disclosures.
- Change notice, acceptance and version retention.
- Suspension, appeal, export, exit and sunset.
- Accessibility/localization and professional translation review.
- Counsel approval and signed publication authorization.
