## Scope

This draft covers YNX informational, Testnet, developer and specifically
identified product services. It does not authorize monitoring or enforcement
beyond applicable law, final terms, privacy notice and product authority.

## Prohibited activity

Users must not use YNX services to:

- gain unauthorized access, scan/exploit systems without authorization, spread
  malware, steal credentials or interfere with availability;
- obtain or disclose seeds, private keys, credentials or personal/confidential
  data without authority;
- commit fraud, deception, impersonation, theft, sanctions evasion, money
  laundering, terrorist financing or other unlawful financial activity;
- manipulate markets, wash trade, fabricate volume/liquidity, front-run through
  unauthorized access or misrepresent prices/performance;
- evade rate, quota, fee, scope, permission, custody, geographic or safety limits;
- submit unlawful, infringing, exploitative, abusive or privacy-violating content;
- harass, threaten, dox or coordinate harm;
- create misleading claims of affiliation, issuer support, audit, approval,
  reserves, returns, production status or public evidence;
- abuse AI to bypass approval, generate/expose secrets, automate prohibited acts
  or present model output as authoritative state; or
- interfere with evidence, audit, appeal, correction, retention or incident
  processes.

## Security research

Good-faith research requires an approved vulnerability-disclosure/safe-harbor
policy with scope, testing limits, prohibited data access, reporting channel,
confidentiality, response and reward terms. No safe harbor is granted by this
draft alone.

## Automated access

Automation must use documented interfaces, respect authentication, scopes, rate
limits, privacy, robots/index permissions and provider terms. It must stop on
revocation, expiry, outage or unsafe unknown state. Scraping or bulk access must
not defeat access controls or redistribute restricted provider/user data.

## Financial and asset activity

Users must not treat Testnet assets as guaranteed value, exploit Faucet or
subsidies, manipulate Testnet evidence into production claims, use unapproved
bridge/stablecoin/custody routes or exceed a signed strategy mandate.

## Content and communications

Final product rules must distinguish private communications, public content,
developer code, marketplace listings and evidence submissions. Removal must use
defined authority, reason, scope, notice where lawful, appeal and correction.

## Enforcement

Potential responses include rate limiting, content restriction, session/token
revocation, account/product suspension, evidence preservation and legally valid
referral. Measures must be necessary, proportionate, scoped, time-bounded where
appropriate and auditable. Emergency action requires later review.

No enforcement may seize native YNXT or change Wallet ownership without an
approved protocol/legal authority that does not currently exist in this draft.

## Appeal and correction

Users require a durable route to understand the reason, submit evidence, appeal,
receive status/outcome and request correction, subject to lawful confidentiality
and security limits. Prior records remain auditable when corrected.

## Reporting

Abuse/security/legal reporting contacts and response targets are not approved.
Reports must not contain seed phrases, private keys or complete secrets.
