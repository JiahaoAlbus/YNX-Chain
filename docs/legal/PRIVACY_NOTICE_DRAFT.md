## Important notice

This draft is not an effective privacy notice and is not legal advice. It must be
reconciled with the exact deployed data flows, legal entity, jurisdictions,
vendors, contracts, retention, user rights and product-specific notices.

## 1. Scope and controller

The intended scope is the YNX Chain informational site, YNX Testnet documentation
and specifically identified products. The responsible controller/business/legal
entity, address, privacy contact and representative are not approved. Products
with independent entities or providers require separate role allocation.

## 2. Data categories

Depending on the selected product, data may include:

- public chain account, transaction, contract and validator data;
- Wallet approval/session/device public keys, challenges, scopes and revocation;
- account/profile/contact or support information the user supplies;
- product objects such as payment intents, orders, strategy mandates, files,
  messages, cases, appeals and audit records;
- device, browser, network, request/error/audit identifiers and security events;
- provider/model, selected AI context classes, usage and cost metadata;
- cookies/local storage or analytics only if separately approved and disclosed;
- KYC/KYB, sanctions or payment data only for a product with an approved legal
  basis, provider and notice; and
- derived/cache/estimate data labeled by source and freshness.

Seed phrases and raw private keys are not required and must not be collected.

## 3. Sources

Sources may include the user, public chain, canonical Wallet, YNX product state,
approved provider, business/customer administrator, security telemetry and
legally valid request. AI inference and third-party data remain labeled inputs,
not authoritative facts about a person.

## 4. Purposes and lawful bases

Potential purposes include providing requested Testnet/product functions,
authentication/security, fraud/abuse prevention, support, reliability, legal
compliance, user-approved AI assistance and product improvement.

The specific lawful basis or statutory category for each purpose/jurisdiction is
not approved. Consent must be freely given, specific, informed and withdrawable
where used. Contract, legitimate-interest, legal-obligation and other bases
require documented necessity and balancing where applicable.

## 5. Public blockchain data

Blockchain records may be public, replicated and difficult or impossible for YNX
to delete unilaterally. The final notice must explain what YNX controls, what it
indexes/displays and what remains protocol/public data. Off-chain profiles should
avoid unnecessary linkage to public addresses.

## 6. AI data

Before an AI call, users see selected context classes, provider/model status,
purpose, estimated resource/cost and applicable retention/training terms. Raw
prompts and sensitive context must not enter logs unnecessarily. Provider failure
must not be replaced by a fabricated provider-backed answer.

## 7. Sharing and processors

Sharing may involve infrastructure, database/cache/storage, AI, email,
monitoring/security, identity/compliance, payment, custody, bridge/oracle and
professional advisers only after provider-register, contract, purpose,
jurisdiction, retention, security and data-rights review.

No list is final. The current provider register is an engineering inventory, not
proof of approved processing.

## 8. International transfers

Hosting/provider regions and transfer mechanisms are not approved. The final
notice must identify transfer locations and applicable safeguards/assessments.

## 9. Retention

Retention must be defined by data class and purpose, including active account,
security/audit, support, financial/compliance, backups, public index/cache, AI,
content and legal hold. Indefinite retention is not a default. Backups require
expiry and deletion propagation, subject to legal and technical constraints.

## 10. Security

Controls include least privilege, key separation, encryption where appropriate,
mode-restricted secrets/state, authentication, audit, monitoring, backup/restore,
incident response and supply-chain review. No system is risk-free, and controls
do not constitute an independent audit.

## 11. Rights and choices

The final notice must explain applicable access, correction, deletion, restriction,
objection, portability, consent withdrawal, appeal and regulator complaint rights;
identity verification; response timing; exceptions; and public-blockchain limits.

Product settings must provide export/delete and permission/session revocation
where applicable. Deleting an off-chain profile does not erase independently
committed public-chain history.

## 12. Children

Age scope and child/minor policy are not approved. No public launch should target
or knowingly process children’s data without jurisdiction-specific review,
appropriate consent/rights and product controls.

## 13. Automated decisions

AI/model scores, labels and recommendations must not independently make legal or
similarly significant decisions. Any consequential workflow requires defined
authority, explanation, human review, evidence, appeal and correction.

## 14. Cookies and tracking

No cookie/analytics deployment is approved by this draft. Before use, complete a
tracker inventory, purpose/lawful-basis analysis, consent controls where required,
retention, provider contracts and opt-out/withdrawal behavior. Essential storage
must remain separated from optional analytics/marketing.

## 15. Incidents

The final program requires detection, containment, evidence preservation, risk
assessment, regulator/user notification where required, recovery, correction and
post-incident review. Privacy/security contacts and response timelines remain to
be approved.

## 16. Changes and contact

Material changes require version, date, notice and consent where required. A
durable privacy contact and applicable regulator/representative information must
exist before effectiveness.

## Acceptance checklist

- Controller/entity and product role mapping.
- Data-flow/record-of-processing inventory.
- Purpose and lawful basis by data class/jurisdiction.
- Provider/processors, contracts and transfer safeguards.
- Retention and deletion schedule, including backups.
- Rights workflow and identity verification.
- Cookie/tracker decision and consent implementation.
- Children/minor and sensitive-data treatment.
- AI context/provider/training/retention disclosure.
- Incident and regulator notification process.
- Professional translation/accessibility and counsel approval.
