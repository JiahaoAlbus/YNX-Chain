# Trust, Appeals, and Market Integrity

Version: 0.1.0-candidate  
Last reviewed: 2026-07-22  
Source commit: `719e1018267ed5a53e6fae5211c5fd8a1503c35c`

YNX Trust records are advisory evidence and governance workflow. A Trust label has no authority to freeze, seize, transfer, or burn native YNXT. Legal or provider controls over separately issued/custodied assets must be attributed to that issuer/provider and its approved process.

## Evidence and labels

Records identify subject, purpose, scope, source, evidence hash/reference, label type, bounded risk weight, confidence, issue/expiry time, review requirement, policy version, and audit hash. Evidence bodies remain in access-controlled storage when public disclosure would expose personal, security, or legally restricted information. A label must distinguish allegation, automated signal, reviewed finding, and final disposition.

Minimum-necessary and purpose-limited review is required. Bulk or unreasonable tracking requests are rejected or escalated. A third-party risk score is not a YNX fact and cannot silently become an asset restriction.

## Appeals

The subject receives a stable case/reference, reason category, material consequence, evidence summary where lawful, submission route, expected review window, and independent escalation path. Appeals support correction evidence and record reviewer, decision, reason, time, policy version, and effect on labels. Retaliation, undisclosed blacklists, and permanent labels without review/expiry are prohibited.

Emergency restrictions, if legally and technically applicable to a non-native provider-controlled asset, must be scoped, time-bounded, logged, reviewable, and linked to authority. They do not expand to native YNXT.

## Market-integrity controls

Prohibit wash trading, spoofing, layering, self-dealing without disclosure, fake volume/liquidity, misleading price or PnL, hidden spread, undisclosed preferential flow, and manipulation of benchmarks/oracles. Surveillance inputs require source/coverage/latency disclosure and human review for material action. Controls must account for false positives and adversarial evasion.

Public transparency reporting should aggregate requests, labels, appeals, reversals, response times, and major policy changes without identifying users or exposing investigation methods. Statistics require period, scope, exclusions, and evidence source.

## Current evidence boundary

Local APIs test evidence creation, advisory labels, appeals and resolution, minimum-necessary tracking review, transparency records, and rejection/classification of abusive requests. This is not proof of a staffed production review body, legal authority, public deployment, or independent oversight.
