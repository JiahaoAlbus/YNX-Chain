## Direct answer

YNX Web4 is the application ecosystem powered by YNX Chain. It connects
independent products through explicit Wallet approval, product-scoped sessions,
authoritative chain state and evidence-linked APIs. It is not one super-app and
does not give every application shared custody or unrestricted permissions.

## What “Web4” means in YNX

The term describes five intended properties:

1. users control their account authority;
2. applications request bounded permissions;
3. important results are verifiable against the defined authority;
4. automation and AI produce evidence and reviewable proposals; and
5. sensitive actions require explicit human and cryptographic approval.

This is YNX product terminology, not a claim of an adopted Internet standard.

## Independent product model

Wallet handles custody and signing. Social handles social identity and
interaction. Pay handles payment intents and receipts. Explorer presents derived
chain views. Developer provides development workflows. Exchange, DEX and Quant
have separate asset, venue and mandate boundaries. Trust handles evidence and
appeal. Cloud and other products retain their own storage and permission models.

No product may obtain a user seed phrase, arbitrary withdrawal authority or owner
change merely because it belongs to YNX Web4.

## Shared trust path

The target shared path binds product identity, bundle identity, device, account,
scope, expiry, challenge, approval, introspection, revocation and audit. Replay,
tampering, wrong product, wrong bundle, wrong device, scope widening, expiry and
revocation must fail closed.

Product candidates are not called integrated until the accepted central protocol
and its negative vectors pass for that product.

## AI boundary

AI can draft, explain, translate, summarize, research, preview, simulate and
propose. It cannot independently sign, pay, trade, withdraw, publish, delete,
change permissions, operate Treasury or upgrade consensus. Context consent,
provider/model status, preview, approval/rejection, audit and failure truth are
required.

## Design and accessibility

The ecosystem uses Klein Blue `#002FA7`, white and neutral black/gray. Products
remain visually related but task-specific. Required languages are English,
Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, Japanese, Korean, Spanish, French,
German, Portuguese, Russian, Arabic and Indonesian. Arabic requires true RTL.

## Current status

YNX Web4 includes components at different maturity levels. Local tests, preview
packages, operator evidence, public deployment, production signing and store
release must be reported independently for each product.
