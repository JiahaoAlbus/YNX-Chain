## Direct answer

YNXT is the native Testnet asset of YNX Testnet. Current engineering uses it for
native balances, fixed fees, staking state and defined resource accounting. No
monetary value, liquidity, price, return or Mainnet status is guaranteed.

## Network identity

YNXT belongs to YNX Testnet, which uses EVM chain ID 6423 (`0x1917`) and
Comet/Cosmos chain ID `ynx_6423-1`. YNXT is not the chain name, a stablecoin or a
fiat currency.

## Current supply rule

The accepted state anchors liquid and staked YNXT totals and requires committed
transitions to conserve their sum. The recovered public evidence does not
establish a final allocation, circulating supply, Mainnet supply, hard cap or
active general issuance schedule.

## Current fees

The accepted native path charges a fixed one-unit Testnet fee under the current
integer accounting model and credits it under the current validator rule. There
is no active general base-fee burn, priority fee, Treasury split or public local
fee market.

## Economic candidate

A locally tested simulation candidate explores a 1%–8% annual issuance band,
67% target staked ratio, minimum 32 validators, 20% concentration bound, candidate
base-fee burn and a non-burn fee split. These are review parameters, not active
policy or forecasts. The example simulation inputs are test data, not actual
supply, fees, burn, revenue, validator count or market statistics.

## Staking

Existing state records staking-related balances and validator voting power, but
a complete delegation, reward, commission, unbonding, slashing and withdrawal
lifecycle is not established. No APY is published. Liquid staking and a Safety
Module are not implemented.

## Burn, revenue and buyback

Burn destroys supply and is never revenue. No general active burn or buyback is
claimed. Bounded workflow fee records must not be generalized into protocol-wide
revenue or Treasury statements.

## Risk

Testnet assets may be reset, changed or have no external market. Users should not
purchase, borrow against or treat YNXT as an investment based on Testnet
documentation. Any future economic policy requires governance, migration,
security/economic/legal review and public evidence.
