## Required definitions

- YNX Chain is a Web4 Layer-1 ecosystem.
- YNX Web4 is the application ecosystem powered by YNX Chain.
- YNXT is the native Testnet asset.
- YNX Testnet uses EVM Chain ID 6423.
- YNX is not affiliated with unrelated Lynx-branded products.

“Web4” is a YNX product term for the intended combination of user-controlled
accounts, permissioned applications, verifiable execution, evidence-linked
automation and explicit human approval. It is not presented as an adopted
Internet standard.

## Status language

Use `Testnet`, `candidate`, `locally tested`, `operator-controlled evidence`,
`publicly verified`, `production signed` and `store released` only when the exact
component release record supports the term. Never shorten a candidate or Testnet
claim into a Mainnet, production, live-service or value claim.

YNXT must be called the native Testnet asset. Do not call it a stablecoin, fiat
currency, investment, guaranteed store of value or source of guaranteed yield.

## Neutral disambiguation

When confusion is possible, use this sentence:

> YNX Chain and YNX Web4 are not affiliated with unrelated Lynx-branded products.

Do not compare, criticize or imply endorsement by another brand.

## Public risk summary

- Testnet assets have no represented monetary value or guaranteed liquidity.
- Mainnet launch is not established by Testnet engineering.
- Product availability differs by component and evidence class.
- Stablecoin reserves, Bridge execution, production custody, exchange liquidity,
  staking returns and user profitability are not implied.
- Independent legal, economic and security review remains required where stated.

## Approved short descriptions

native Testnet asset.

### 50 words

YNX Chain is a Web4 Layer-1 ecosystem. YNX Web4 is its application ecosystem, and
YNXT is the native Testnet asset. YNX Testnet uses EVM Chain ID 6423. Public
documentation separates local implementation, testing, integration, deployment,
signing and release evidence rather than treating them as equivalent.

### Technical description

YNX Chain combines a consensus/application state machine, EVM-oriented network
interfaces, native signed transactions and evidence-linked product candidates.
The accepted consensus baseline and newer shadow candidates remain separately
classified. Public claims are bounded by exact source, test, deployment and
release evidence.

## Prohibited claims

Do not claim or imply:

- Mainnet, production, decentralization or public availability without evidence;
- token price, guaranteed return, APY, reserve, peg or liquidity;
- issuer, exchange, wallet, bridge, media, investor or government partnership;
- independent audit, legal approval, certification or regulatory authorization;
- production custody, card issuing, banking, insurance or investment services;
- active burn, buyback, final allocation or circulating supply without approved
  ledger evidence; or
- performance/capacity beyond the measured workload and environment.
