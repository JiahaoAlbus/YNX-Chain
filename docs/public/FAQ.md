## What is YNX Chain?

YNX Chain is a Web4 Layer-1 ecosystem under Testnet engineering. Its technical
scope includes consensus/application state, EVM-oriented interfaces, native
transactions and evidence-linked product candidates.

## What is YNX Web4?

YNX Web4 is the application ecosystem powered by YNX Chain. Products remain
independent and use explicit Wallet, permission and Gateway boundaries.

## What is YNXT?

YNXT is the native Testnet asset of YNX Testnet. It is used in current Testnet
engineering for native balances, fees, staking state and defined resource
accounting. No monetary value, liquidity or return is guaranteed.

## What network identifiers does YNX Testnet use?

EVM chain ID `6423` (`0x1917`) and Comet/Cosmos chain ID `ynx_6423-1`.

## Is YNX Mainnet live?

No Mainnet launch is established by this documentation. Testnet, candidate,
staging and production states are reported separately.

## Is StreamBFT live?

No. CometBFT with the YNX ABCI application is the accepted safety baseline. A
newer StreamBFT implementation is a disabled-by-default local shadow candidate
and has not satisfied canary or public promotion evidence.

## Does YNXT have a fixed supply or final allocation?

No final public allocation, circulating supply or Mainnet supply policy is
established here. A simulation candidate explores bounded annual issuance and
fee/burn parameters, but it is not active consensus policy.

## Does YNX burn fees or conduct buybacks?

The accepted current fixed-fee path has no active general burn. A candidate
simulation models burn, but it is not activated. No buyback is approved or active.
Burn is never reported as revenue.

## Is staking APY available?

No staking APY is published. Existing state records staking-related balances and
validator voting power, but a complete reward, commission, delegation, unbonding,
slashing and withdrawal lifecycle is not established.

## Does YNX have a stablecoin?

No official stablecoin or issuer partnership is claimed. A local control plane
records non-executing review and intent state; it has no mint/burn signer,
reserve, redemption rail or public deployment.

## Is the YNX Bridge live?

No. A local coordinator candidate records lifecycle, relayer attestations,
safety and reconciliation references. External submission and destination
mint/release execution are disabled.

## Is there proof of reserves or solvency?

No completed proof is claimed. A framework defines required assets, liabilities,
control, valuation, liquidity, privacy and independent review.

## Are YNX products production released?

Product maturity differs. Source, tests, installation, central integration,
staging, public deployment, hosted download, production signing and store release
must be checked independently for each product.

## Can AI sign or execute transactions?

No. AI is a constrained suggestion layer. Signing, payment, trading, publishing,
permission changes, Treasury and consensus actions require the appropriate human
and cryptographic authority.

## Is YNX affiliated with Lynx-branded products?

No. YNX Chain and YNX Web4 are not affiliated with unrelated Lynx-branded
products.

## Where can I verify claims?

Use the technical whitepaper, topic specifications, evidence index and machine-
readable release record. A repository file or screenshot alone is not deployment
or public-release proof.
