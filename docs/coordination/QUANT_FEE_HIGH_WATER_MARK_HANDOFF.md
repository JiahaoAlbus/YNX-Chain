# Quant Fee and High-Water-Mark Handoff

Ownership remains with Chain Core and Quant Lab; Tokenomics does not copy or activate their asset-authority code. The recovered Chain Core implementation is commit `fde0f55` (an ancestor of `codex/final-chain-core`) at `internal/assetauth/vault.go`, with direct test `TestPerformanceFeeUsesRealizedNetProfitAbovePersistentHighWaterMark`. It was reverified locally with `go test ./internal/assetauth -run PerformanceFee -count=1`.

The contract is: realized net PnL equals realized gross PnL less trading, funding and provider costs; performance fee applies only to positive cumulative realized net PnL above the persistent prior high-water mark; losses never charge or reset the mark; recovery below that mark cannot charge again; management fee is time-proportional to average NAV; rates, duration and arithmetic overflow fail closed. The canonical vector produces realized net 800, cumulative 2,800, eligible base 300, 20% performance fee 60, annual 2% management fee 200, and new mark 2,800. A subsequent net loss of 120 leaves the mark at 2,800 and charges zero; a 100 recovery still charges zero.

Quant Lab commit `0e1810b` separately reconciles PnL attribution and explicitly reports management/performance fees unsupported in its current runtime. DEX v1 requires performance fee fields to remain zero. Consequently the invariant is implemented and tested in the owning Chain Core branch, but it is not integrated into this branch, an enabled managed Vault, consensus settlement, Wallet consent, billing ledger or public deployment. All related integration and deployment booleans remain false.

Activation requires a versioned managed-Vault mandate with prior user consent, fee asset/recipient, persistent state and migration, exact fee ledger category, loss/recovery vectors, withdrawal/emergency exit, Wallet preview and revoke, governance/timelock where applicable, independent audit, and Explorer-visible assessment/collection receipts. Unrealized profit, hidden spread, reset fees and strategy-engine withdrawal authority remain prohibited.
